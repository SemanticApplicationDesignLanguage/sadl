The SADL Intermediate Form

Last revised 11/20/2017 by A. W. Crapo.

Introduction

The SADL Intermediate Form is an in-memory representation used to capture the semantics of graph patterns and functions used in SADL rules, queries, and tests. This in-memory representation serves several purposes.

  1. It serves as the input to target reasoner-specific translators that then convert rules and queries into the format required by the reasoner. For example, a Jena-based reasoner requires that rules be expressed in Jena Rule langiage and that queries be expressed in SPARQL. Hence a compatible Jena-based translator will translate the intermediate form into Jena Rules and SPARQL queries.
  2. It serves as the mechanism for capturing and preserving tests and other SADL "commands" during model processing so that they can be processed during inferencing of a SADL model.
  3. Expressions in the Intermediate Form can be serialized for examination by users (the Explain command) and for automated testing.
