Notes on SADL Grammar

The portion of the SADL grammar that is used across rules, queries, and tests is the Expression. Expression also implements the precedence of operators. The hierarchy goes like this:

Expression

    OrExpression ("||" or "or")

        AndExpression ("&&" or "and")

            RelationalExpression ("=" or "= =" or "is" or "!=" or "is not" or "is only" or "is not only" or "<" or "not <" or "<=" or
                                                "not <=" or ">" or "not >" or ">=" or "not >=")

                AdditiveExpression ("+" or "-")

                    MultiplicativeExpression ("*" or "/" or "^" or "%")

                        UnaryOrPrimaryExpression ("!" or "-" or ParentheticalExpression or Builtin or GraphPattern or
                                                 IntervalValue or ExplicitValue or ValueTable

 

where

    ParentheticalExpression is an Expression in parentheses, e.g., (2+3)

    Builtin is a function name with a list of 0 or more comma-separated arguments in parentheses, e.g., getDataValue(s, p, 0)

    GraphPattern is PropOfSubj or SubjProp or InstAttrSPV or InstAttrPSV or MergedTriples

    IntervalValue is a relational operator (as used in RelationalExpression) followed by an AdditiveExpression

    ExplicitValue is a name, e.g., "George" or a literal, e.g., "3.14.159", or "PI" or "known"

    ValueTable is a ValueRow or "{ValueRow, ValueRow, ...}"   (one or more ValueRows)

    ValueRow is "[ExplicitValue, ExplicitValue, ....]"    (one or more ExplicitValues)
